Day 6: Katyayani Maa

Date

Goddess Katyayani is worshipped on the 6th day of Navratri this year it is on 9th October Wednesday.
During Navratri, 9 forms of Goddess Durga are worshiped with rituals.

Story

To destroy demon Mahishasura, Goddess Parvati took the form of Goddess Katyayani. It was the most violent form of Goddess Parvati. In this form Goddess Parvati is also known as Warrior Goddess.

It is believed that the planet Brihaspati is governed by Goddess Katyayani.

Goddess Katyayani rides on the magnificent lion and depicted with four hands. Goddess Katyayani carries lotus flower and sword in her left hands and keeps her right hands in Abhaya and Varada Mudras.
According to religious texts Goddess Parvati was born at the home of sage Katya and due to which this form of Goddess Parvati is known as Katyayani.

Importance

Day 6 of Navratri is dedicated to Goddess Katyayani, the fierce and powerful form of Durga. She is worshipped as the warrior goddess, symbolizing strength, courage, and protection. It is believed that she was born to Sage Katyayan after his penance to destroy the demon Mahishasura. Devotees pray to Goddess Katyayani to remove negativity, overcome fears, and gain victory over adversities. The color of the day is grey, representing balance, wisdom, and inner strength. Worshipping her is said to bring success in personal and professional endeavors, as well as remove obstacles in marriage and relationships.

Mantra

॥ॐ देवी कात्यायन्यै नमः॥

॥ Om Devi Katyayanyai Namah॥

(Meaning: Om, I bow to Goddess Katyayani Maa)

Prarthana

॥ चन्द्रहासोज्ज्वलकरा शार्दूलवरवाहना।
कात्यायनी शुभं दद्याद् देवी दानवघातिनी॥

॥Chandrahasojjvalakara Shardulavaravahana।
Katyayani Shubham Dadyad Devi Danavaghatini॥

Stuti

॥या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ कात्यायनी रूपेण संस्थिता। नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः॥

Facts

  • Chandraghanta is associated with peace, courage, and the destruction of evil.
  • She adorns a crescent moon on her forehead, which resembles a bell (ghanta), symbolizing her name.
  • Her color is golden, representing strength, grace, and bravery.
  • Chandraghanta is often depicted riding a lion, symbolizing fearlessness and the readiness to combat evil forces.
  • She carries weapons such as a trident, mace, bow, and arrow in her ten hands, demonstrating her warrior form.
  • Devotees believe that praying to Chandraghanta brings peace, protection, and relief from worldly sorrows.
  • She represents the Manipura (solar plexus) chakra, which is linked to energy, power, and self-confidence.
  • Devotees offer red flowers and sweets to please Goddess Chandraghanta.
  • Praying to her on this day is believed to remove obstacles and help devotees achieve tranquility and spiritual growth.
  • Today's color to wear is Grey.
