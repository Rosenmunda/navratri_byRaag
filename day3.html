Day 3: Chandraghanta Maa

Date

Goddess Chandraghanta is worshipped on the third day of Navratri this year it is on 5th October Saturday.
During Navratri, 9 forms of Goddess Durga are worshiped with rituals.

Story

Goddess Chandraghanta is the married form the Goddess Parvati. After getting married to Lord Shiva Goddess Mahagauri started adorning her forehead with half Chandra and due to which Goddess Parvati was known as Goddess Chandraghanta.

It is believed that the planet Shukra is governed by Goddess Chandraghanta.

Goddess Chandraghanta mounts on the tigress. She wears the semi-circular moon (Chandra) on her forehead. The half-moon on her forehead looks like the bell (Ghanta) and because of that she is known as Chandra-Ghanta. She is depicted with ten hands. Goddess Chandraghanta carries Trishul, Gada, Sword and Kamandal in her four left hands and keeps the fifth left hand in Varada Mudra. She carries lotus flower, Arrow, Dhanush and Japa Mala in her four right hands and keeps the fifth right hand in Abhaya Mudra.

This form of Goddess Parvati is peaceful and for welfare of her devotees. In this form Goddess Chandraghanta is ready for the war with all her weapons. It is believed that the sound of the moon-bell on her forehead expel all type of spirits away from her devotees.

Importance

Day 3 of Navratri is dedicated to Goddess Chandraghanta, the fierce and warrior form of Goddess Parvati. She symbolizes courage, bravery, and protection, with a half-moon (Chandra) on her forehead in the shape of a bell (Ghanta), which gives her the name Chandraghanta. Devotees believe she wards off negative energies and evil spirits, offering her blessings of inner strength and fearlessness. Worshipping her brings peace and harmony, as well as the ability to fight life's adversities with determination. The day emphasizes the balance between fearlessness and tranquility, guiding devotees towards mental peace and spiritual elevation.

Mantra

॥ ॐ देवी चन्द्रघण्टायै नमः॥ ॥

॥ Om Devi Chandraghantayai Namah ॥

(Meaning: Om, I bow to Goddess Chandraghanta Maa)

Prarthana

॥ पिण्डज प्रवरारूढा चण्डकोपास्त्रकैर्युता।
प्रसादं तनुते मह्यम् चन्द्रघण्टेति विश्रुता॥

॥ Pindaja Pravararudha Chandakopastrakairyuta।
Prasadam Tanute Mahyam Chandraghanteti Vishruta॥

Stuti

॥या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ चन्द्रघण्टा रूपेण संस्थिता। नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः॥

॥Ya Devi Sarvabhuteshu Maa Chandraghanta Rupena Samsthita।
Namastasyai Namastasyai Namastasyai Namo Namah॥


Dhyana

॥ वन्दे वाञ्छितलाभाय चन्द्रार्धकृतशेखराम्।
सिंहारूढा चन्द्रघण्टा यशस्विनीम्॥
मणिपुर स्थिताम् तृतीय दुर्गा त्रिनेत्राम्।
खङ्ग, गदा, त्रिशूल, चापशर, पद्म कमण्डलु माला वराभीतकराम्॥
पटाम्बर परिधानां मृदुहास्या नानालङ्कार भूषिताम्।
मञ्जीर, हार, केयूर, किङ्किणि, रत्नकुण्डल मण्डिताम॥
प्रफुल्ल वन्दना बिबाधारा कान्त कपोलाम् तुगम् कुचाम्।
कमनीयां लावण्यां क्षीणकटि नितम्बनीम्॥


॥ Vande Vanchhitalabhaya Chandrardhakritashekharam।
Simharudha Chandraghanta Yashasvinim॥
Manipura Sthitam Tritiya Durga Trinetram।
Khanga, Gada, Trishula, Chapashara, Padma Kamandalu Mala Varabhitakaram॥
Patambara Paridhanam Mriduhasya Nanalankara Bhushitam।
Manjira, Hara, Keyura, Kinkini, Ratnakundala Manditam॥
Praphulla Vandana Bibadhara Kanta Kapolam Tugam Kucham।
Kamaniyam Lavanyam Kshinakati Nitambanim॥


Stotra

॥ आपदुध्दारिणी त्वंहि आद्या शक्तिः शुभपराम्।
अणिमादि सिद्धिदात्री चन्द्रघण्टे प्रणमाम्यहम्॥
चन्द्रमुखी इष्ट दात्री इष्टम् मन्त्र स्वरूपिणीम्।
धनदात्री, आनन्ददात्री चन्द्रघण्टे प्रणमाम्यहम्॥
नानारूपधारिणी इच्छामयी ऐश्वर्यदायिनीम्।
सौभाग्यारोग्यदायिनी चन्द्रघण्टे प्रणमाम्यहम्॥


॥Apaduddharini Tvamhi Adya Shaktih Shubhparam।
Animadi Siddhidatri Chandraghante Pranamamyaham॥
Chandramukhi Ishta Datri Ishtam Mantra Swarupinim।
Dhanadatri, Anandadatri Chandraghante Pranamamyaham॥
Nanarupadharini Ichchhamayi Aishwaryadayinim।
Saubhagyarogyadayini Chandraghante Pranamamyaham॥

Facts

  • Chandraghanta is associated with peace, courage, and the destruction of evil.
  • She adorns a crescent moon on her forehead, which resembles a bell (ghanta), symbolizing her name.
  • Her color is golden, representing strength, grace, and bravery.
  • Chandraghanta is often depicted riding a lion, symbolizing fearlessness and the readiness to combat evil forces.
  • She carries weapons such as a trident, mace, bow, and arrow in her ten hands, demonstrating her warrior form.
  • Devotees believe that praying to Chandraghanta brings peace, protection, and relief from worldly sorrows.
  • She represents the Manipura (solar plexus) chakra, which is linked to energy, power, and self-confidence.
  • Devotees offer red flowers and sweets to please Goddess Chandraghanta.
  • Praying to her on this day is believed to remove obstacles and help devotees achieve tranquility and spiritual growth.
