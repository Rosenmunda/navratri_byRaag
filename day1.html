Day 1: Shailaputri Maa

Date

According to the Hindu calendar, Shardiya Navratri is starting from 3rd October Thrusday.
During Navratri, 9 forms of Goddess Durga are worshiped with rituals.

Story

On the first day of Navratri, Maa Shailaputri is worshipped, symbolizing purity and strength. She is the daughter of the Himalayas and is considered an embodiment of the collective power of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Riding a bull and holding a trident and lotus, she represents nature and the energy that sustains life. Devotees seek her blessings for spiritual awakening and a strong foundation for the days ahead.

The story of Maa Shailaputri begins with her previous incarnation as Goddess Sati, the daughter of King Daksha and wife of Lord Shiva. When Daksha insulted Shiva by not inviting him to a grand yajna, Sati, unable to bear the disrespect, immolated herself in the fire. Reborn as Shailaputri, the daughter of the Himalayas (Shaila means mountain), she symbolizes strength and devotion. In this new form, she reunited with Lord Shiva after intense penance. Her story represents the divine cycle of life, death, and rebirth, and her worship on Navratri's first day is believed to instill stability and devotion.

Importance

Day 1 of Navratri is dedicated to Maa Shailaputri, who represents strength, purity, and devotion. Worshipping her marks the beginning of the spiritual journey through Navratri. She is believed to grant stability and focus, laying a strong foundation for the following days of the festival. Devotees seek her blessings for a pure heart and a calm mind, symbolizing the first step towards spiritual awakening and growth. Her importance also lies in her embodiment of nature's power, connecting devotees to the natural world.

Mantra

॥ ॐ देवी शैलपुत्र्यै नमः ॥

॥ Om Devi Shailaputryai Namah ॥

(Meaning: Om, I bow to Goddess Shailaputri)

Prarthana

॥ वन्दे वाञ्छितलाभाय चन्द्रार्धकृतशेखराम्।
वृषारूढां शूलधरां शैलपुत्रीं यशस्विनीम् ॥

॥ Vande Vanchhitalabhaya Chandrardhakritashekharam।
Vrisharudham Shuladharam Shailaputrim Yashasvinim॥

Stuti

॥ या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ शैलपुत्री रूपेण संस्थिता। नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः॥

॥Ya Devi Sarvabhuteshu Maa Shailaputri Rupena Samsthita।
Namastasyai Namastasyai Namastasyai Namo Namah॥

Dhyana

॥ वन्दे वाञ्छितलाभाय चन्द्रार्धकृतशेखराम्।
वृषारूढां शूलधरां शैलपुत्रीं यशस्विनीम्॥
पूणेन्दु निभाम् गौरी मूलाधार स्थिताम् प्रथम दुर्गा त्रिनेत्राम्।
पटाम्बर परिधानां रत्नाकिरीटा नामालंकार भूषिता॥
प्रफुल्ल वन्दना पल्लवाधरां कान्त कपोलाम् तुगम् कुचाम्।
कमनीयां लावण्यां स्नेमुखी क्षीणमध्यां नितम्बनीम्॥

॥ Vande Vanchhitalabhaya Chandrardhakritashekharam।
Vrisharudham Shuladharam Shailaputrim Yashasvinim॥
Punendu Nibham Gauri Muladhara Sthitam Prathama Durga Trinetram।
Patambara Paridhanam Ratnakirita Namalankara Bhushita॥
Praphulla Vandana Pallavadharam Kanta Kapolam Tugam Kucham।
Kamaniyam Lavanyam Snemukhi Kshinamadhyam Nitambanim॥ ॥

Stotra

॥ प्रथम दुर्गा त्वंहि भवसागरः तारणीम्।
धन ऐश्वर्य दायिनी शैलपुत्री प्रणमाम्यहम्॥
त्रिलोजननी त्वंहि परमानन्द प्रदीयमान्।
सौभाग्यरोग्य दायिनी शैलपुत्री प्रणमाम्यहम्॥
चराचरेश्वरी त्वंहि महामोह विनाशिनीं।
मुक्ति भुक्ति दायिनीं शैलपुत्री प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ॥

॥Prathama Durga Tvamhi Bhavasagarah Taranim।
Dhana Aishwarya Dayini Shailaputri Pranamamyaham॥
Trilojanani Tvamhi Paramananda Pradiyaman।
Saubhagyarogya Dayini Shailaputri Pranamamyaham॥
Charachareshwari Tvamhi Mahamoha Vinashinim।
Mukti Bhukti Dayinim Shailaputri Pranamamyaham॥ ॥

Facts

  • Shailaputri is associated with the moon and rules the Muladhara (root) chakra.
  • Her color is white, symbolizing purity and new beginnings.
  • She is often depicted holding a lotus in her right hand and a trident in her left.
  • Worshippers often offer jasmine flowers and ghee to Shailaputri.
  • Incarnation Shailputri is considered to be an incarnation of Mahakali, and a reincarnation of Sati, Shiva's first wife
  • Daughter of the mountain Shailputri is the daughter of the mountain king, Himavat, and her name translates to "daughter of the mountain"
  • Symbolism Shailputri is white in color, which represents purity, innocence, peace, and calmness. She rides a white bull, which symbolizes righteousness and strength. She carries a Trishul in her right hand and a lotus flower in her left
  • Prayer Devotees pray to Shailputri for their spiritual awakening and to overcome any bad effects of the moon
  • Wear Yellow color on Thursday and enjoy your Navratri day with a sense of unparalleled optimism and joy. This is a warm color that keeps the person cheerful all day.
