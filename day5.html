Day 5: Skandamata Maa

Date

Goddess Skandamata is worshipped on the 5th day of Navratri this year it is on 8th October Tuseday.
During Navratri, 9 forms of Goddess Durga are worshiped with rituals.

Story

When Goddess Parvati became the mother of Lord Skanda (also known as Lord Kartikeya), Mata Parvati was known as Goddess Skandamata.

It is believed that the planet Budha is governed by Goddess Skandamata.

Goddess Skandamata mounts on the ferocious lion. She carries baby Murugan in her lap. Lord Murugan is also known as Kartikeya and brother of Lord Ganesha. Goddess Skandamata is depicted with four hands. She carries lotus flowers in her upper two hands. She holds baby Murugan in one of her right hand and keeps the other right hand in Abhaya Mudra. She sits on lotus flower and because of that Skandamata is also known as Goddess Padmasana.
The complexion of Goddess Skandamata is Shubhra (शुभ्र) which describes her white complexion. Devotees who worship this form of Goddess Parvati get the benefit of worshipping Lord Kartikeya. This quality is possessed only by Skandamata form of Goddess Parvati.

Importance

Day 5 of Navratri is dedicated to Goddess Skandamata, the mother of Lord Kartikeya (Skanda), the god of war. She represents the strength of motherhood and the nurturing aspect of the divine. Devotees worship her to seek wisdom, power, and prosperity. She is depicted riding a lion, carrying her son Kartikeya in her lap, symbolizing her protective nature. The color associated with this day is white, representing purity, peace, and clarity of mind. Worshipping Skandamata is believed to bring harmony, remove obstacles, and lead devotees on a path of spiritual awakening and success.

Mantra

॥ॐ देवी स्कन्दमातायै नमः॥

॥Om Devi Skandamatayai Namah॥

(Meaning: Om, I bow to Goddess Skandamata Maa)

Prarthana

॥ सिंहासनगता नित्यं पद्माञ्चित करद्वया।
शुभदास्तु सदा देवी स्कन्दमाता यशस्विनी॥

॥Simhasanagata Nityam Padmanchita Karadvaya।
Shubhadastu Sada Devi Skandamata Yashasvini॥

Stuti

॥या देवी सर्वभूतेषु माँ स्कन्दमाता रूपेण संस्थिता। नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः॥

॥Ya Devi Sarvabhuteshu Ma Skandamata Rupena Samsthita।
Namastasyai Namastasyai Namastasyai Namo Namah॥


Dhyana

॥वन्दे वाञ्छित कामार्थे चन्द्रार्धकृतशेखराम्।
सिंहरूढ़ा चतुर्भुजा स्कन्दमाता यशस्विनीम्॥
धवलवर्णा विशुध्द चक्रस्थितों पञ्चम दुर्गा त्रिनेत्राम्।
अभय पद्म युग्म करां दक्षिण उरू पुत्रधराम् भजेम्॥

प्रफुल्ल वन्दना पल्लवाधरां कान्त कपोलाम् पीन पयोधराम्।

Stotra

समग्रतत्वसागरम् पारपारगहराम्॥
शिवाप्रभा समुज्वलां स्फुच्छशागशेखराम्।
ललाटरत्नभास्करां जगत्प्रदीप्ति भास्कराम्॥
महेन्द्रकश्यपार्चितां सनत्कुमार संस्तुताम्।
सुरासुरेन्द्रवन्दिता यथार्थनिर्मलाद्भुताम्॥
अतर्क्यरोचिरूविजां विकार दोषवर्जिताम्।
मुमुक्षुभिर्विचिन्तितां विशेषतत्वमुचिताम्॥
नानालङ्कार भूषिताम् मृगेन्द्रवाहनाग्रजाम्।
सुशुध्दतत्वतोषणां त्रिवेदमार भूषणाम्॥
सुधार्मिकौपकारिणी सुरेन्द्र वैरिघातिनीम्।

जयेश्वरि त्रिलोचने प्रसीद देवी पाहिमाम्॥

Facts

  • Skandamata is worshipped as the mother of Lord Kartikeya (Skanda), the warrior god.
  • She is associated with nurturing, protection, and the embodiment of a mother's love.
  • Skandamata rides a lion and carries her son Kartikeya on her lap, symbolizing fearlessness and maternal care.
  • Her color is white, representing purity, calmness, and serenity.
  • Devotees believe that worshipping Skandamata brings blessings of wisdom, power, and prosperity.
  • Skandamata governs the Vishuddha (throat) chakra, which is linked to communication, truth, and self-expression.
  • Devotees offer yellow flowers and fruits to please the goddess.
  • Praying to Skandamata on this day is believed to remove all kinds of challenges and obstacles in life.
  • Worshipping her brings peace, harmony, and divine grace to the devotees' lives.
  • The color to wear on this day is grey.
